# US Patent and Trademark Office (us-patent-and-trademark-office)
The US Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) is responsible for granting patents and registering trademarks to protect intellectual property in the United States. The USPTO examines patent applications to determine if an invention is new, non-obvious, and useful, and grants patents to those that meet the criteria. They also register trademarks, which are words, phrases, symbols, or designs that distinguish goods or services of one entity from another. The USPTO plays a crucial role in promoting innovation and protecting the rights of inventors and businesses by granting patents and trademarks that provide legal protection for their creations.
